Research Aims

My research, conducted as part of the Antonis Rokas Lab at Vanderbilt University, aims to utilize DNA records to uncover tales of a species’ evolutionary past, present and potential future trajectory. To gain a better understanding of genome evolution, I use organisms from the fungal kingdom. By utilizing whole genome sequencing approaches and computational tools, I am able to analyze sequence evolution, population-level relationships and mutation variation.

Current projects involve wine associated strains of Saccharomyces cerevisiae (baker’s yeast) and the human pathogen Candida lusitaniae (syn. Clavispora lusitaniae). Past work includes study of the primary human and animal pathogen, Cryptococcus gattii.

More generally, I am most interested in how populations evolve, diverge and what traits or genomic elements define them.
