Research Aims

My research, conducted as part of the Antonis Rokas Lab at Vanderbilt University, aims to utilize various 'omics approaches to understand fungal genome evolution. By utilizing whole genome sequencing and computational tools, I am able to analyze sequence evolution, population-level relationships, and mutation variation.

Recent representative publications:
Steenwyk and Rokas (2017) G3
Steenwyk et al. (2016) BMC Genomics

Current projects involve wine associated strains of Saccharomyces cerevisiae (baker’s yeast) and the non-conventional wine yeast genus Hanseniaspora. Past work includes study of the primary human and animal pathogen, Cryptococcus gattii.

More generally, I am most interested in how populations and/or evolve, diverge, and what traits or genomic elements define them.

To enable these analyses, I have learned and implement multiple programming languages such as Python, R, bash, and awk. Numerous scripts that I have written are publically available at among my github repositories. More specifically, these respositories aim to enable the analysis phylogenetic, molecular, and related analyses.
